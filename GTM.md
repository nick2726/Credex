# GTM Strategy - ZenAudit AI

## 1. Target User
**The "Scrappy CTO" or "Finance Ops Lead" at a Series A/B Startup (20-150 employees).**
These companies have enough people that "Shadow AI Spend" (employees individual-billing $20/mo) is starting to hurt, but they aren't big enough to have a procurement team.

## 2. Pre-tool Behavior
They are likely Googling:
- "Cursor vs VS Code Copilot cost for teams"
- "Manage AWS credits for startups"
- "Claude enterprise vs team plan"
- Or scrolling **X (Twitter)** looking at "How I cut my startup's burn" threads.

## 3. Online Hangouts
- **Slack Groups:** TechStars, YC Alumni, Engineering Leadership (LeadDev).
- **Subreddits:** r/startups, r/finance, r/saas.
- **X Lists:** Influential Engineering Managers and VCs (who care about burn rates).

## 4. First 100 Users Strategy ($0 budget)
1. **The "Check My Stack" Thread:** I will identify 5 high-profile "Tech Influencers" on X and reply to their stack threads with a "free audit" of their burn.
2. **Indie Hackers "Show & Tell":** Post a deep-dive "How I built a tool to save startups $10k/yr" on IH.
3. **VC Partner Reachout:** VCs hate waste. DMs to 10 VC Associates/Partners with: "Hey, built this tool for your portfolio founders to sanity-check their AI burn. 100% free, no login."
4. **Tool-Specific Discord/Slack:** Join Cursor/Claude communities and help people in the "pricing" channels by offering the audit link.

## 5. The Unfair Channel
**Credex's existing discount data.**
By showing anonymous benchmarks (e.g., "Companies your size usually pay $X for this"), we tap into the "FOMO" of overpaying. Credex's relationship with vendors allows for unique insights that a solo developer couldn't provide.

## 6. Week 1 Traction Goal
- 500 Unique Visitors.
- 10% Audit Completion Rate (50 Audits).
- 5 consultation bookings for Credex (for high-savings leads).
